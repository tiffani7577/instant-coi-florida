Cheapest Home Insurance in Fort Lauderdale, FL (2026)

Fort Lauderdale homeowners pay some of the highest insurance rates in the country — but there are real strategies to find the best rate for your specific home.

Get Free Quote

Finding cheap home insurance in Fort Lauderdale is genuinely difficult. Broward County's coastal location, hurricane exposure, and the broader Florida insurance crisis mean that Fort Lauderdale homeowners face some of the highest premiums in the country. But "cheapest" doesn't mean impossible — it means knowing where to look, what discounts to claim, and which carriers are currently competing for your business.

What Fort Lauderdale Homeowners Actually Pay in 2026

Let's start with realistic numbers. Here are typical premium ranges for Fort Lauderdale homes in 2026, based on dwelling coverage of $300,000:

  • New construction (built after 2010), hip roof, impact windows: $3,500-$5,500/year
  • Home built 2000-2010, hip roof, panel shutters: $5,000-$7,500/year
  • Home built 1990-2000, gable roof, no shutters: $7,000-$10,000/year
  • Home built before 1990, older roof: $9,000-$14,000+/year

These ranges reflect the reality that your home's construction features matter as much as your location. Two homes on the same street can have premiums that differ by $3,000-$5,000 per year based on roof type, opening protection, and construction quality.

The Carriers Writing Policies in Fort Lauderdale Right Now

The Florida insurance market changes constantly. Carriers that were writing policies in Broward County last year may not be this year, and new carriers enter the market periodically. As of 2026, carriers actively writing new policies in Fort Lauderdale include:

  • Universal Property & Casualty: One of Florida's largest private carriers, generally competitive for homes with newer roofs and good wind mitigation features.
  • Citizens Insurance: The state-backed insurer of last resort, often competitive for older homes or properties that don't qualify for standard private market coverage.
  • Heritage Insurance: Has been actively writing in Broward County and can be competitive for certain property profiles.
  • Slide Insurance: A newer Florida carrier that has been growing its Broward County book and can offer competitive rates for qualifying properties.
  • Surplus lines carriers: For properties that don't qualify for standard market coverage, surplus lines carriers can provide coverage, typically at higher premiums.

The key point: the carrier that's cheapest for your neighbor may not be cheapest for you. Underwriting criteria vary significantly, and the only way to find your best rate is to get quotes from multiple carriers.

The 5 Biggest Discounts Fort Lauderdale Homeowners Miss

1. Wind Mitigation Credits (Most Valuable)

A wind mitigation inspection costs $150-$250 and can reduce your windstorm premium by 20-45%. This is the single highest-ROI action most Fort Lauderdale homeowners can take. If you haven't had an inspection in the past 5 years, get one immediately. The report is valid for 5 years and must be honored by any Florida carrier.

2. Impact Window and Door Credits

If you have impact-resistant windows and doors that meet Florida's High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) standards, you qualify for opening protection credits that can reduce your premium by $800-$2,500 per year. Many Fort Lauderdale homeowners have impact windows but haven't submitted the documentation to their carrier to claim the credit.

3. New Roof Discount

A roof replaced in the past 5 years typically qualifies for a new roof discount that can reduce your premium by $500-$1,500 per year. If you've replaced your roof recently, make sure your carrier has the permit documentation on file.

4. Multi-Policy Discount

Bundling your home and auto insurance with the same carrier typically saves 5-15% on both policies. In Fort Lauderdale, this can translate to $300-$800 in annual savings. However, not all carriers write both home and auto in Florida — ask your agent which carriers offer the best bundle discount for your situation.

5. Annual Payment Discount

Many carriers offer a discount of 3-7% for paying your annual premium in full rather than monthly. On a $7,000 premium, this can save $210-$490 per year with no other changes to your coverage.

The Right Way to Shop for the Cheapest Rate

Shopping for home insurance in Fort Lauderdale is not like shopping for auto insurance. You can't just go to a comparison website and get accurate quotes in 5 minutes. Florida's home insurance market requires a more deliberate approach:

  • Work with an independent agent. An independent agent who works with multiple Florida carriers can access quotes from dozens of companies simultaneously and knows which carriers are currently competitive for your specific property profile.
  • Get at least 3 quotes. Premium differences between carriers for the same property can be $2,000-$4,000 per year. Never accept the first quote.
  • Compare coverage, not just price. The cheapest policy is not always the best value. Make sure you're comparing equivalent coverage amounts, deductibles, and coverage types.
  • Shop every year. Florida's insurance market changes constantly. The carrier that was cheapest last year may not be this year. Set a reminder to shop your coverage 60-90 days before your renewal date.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average home insurance cost in Fort Lauderdale?
The average home insurance premium in Fort Lauderdale in 2026 is approximately $6,800-$9,500 per year for a single-family home with $300,000 in dwelling coverage. This is above the Florida state average due to Fort Lauderdale's coastal location in Broward County. Premiums vary significantly based on your home's age, roof condition, construction type, and distance from the coast.
Which insurance company is cheapest for Fort Lauderdale homes?
There is no single cheapest carrier for all Fort Lauderdale homes — the best rate depends on your specific property. However, carriers that consistently offer competitive rates in Broward County include Universal Property & Casualty, Citizens Insurance, Heritage Insurance, and several surplus lines carriers for higher-risk properties. The only way to find the cheapest option for your specific home is to get multiple quotes through an independent agent.
How can I lower my home insurance premium in Fort Lauderdale?
The most impactful ways to lower your Fort Lauderdale home insurance premium are: (1) Get a wind mitigation inspection — can save $500-$2,000/year, (2) Install impact windows and doors — can save $800-$2,500/year, (3) Replace your roof if it's over 15 years old, (4) Raise your deductible, (5) Bundle with auto insurance, (6) Shop the market annually — rates change significantly year to year.
Is Citizens Insurance the cheapest option in Fort Lauderdale?
Citizens Insurance is often competitive in Fort Lauderdale due to its rate caps, but it is not always the cheapest option. For homes with newer roofs, impact windows, and other favorable features, some private carriers can offer lower premiums than Citizens with broader coverage. For older homes or properties with prior claims, Citizens may indeed be the most affordable option. Always compare both.
Does Fort Lauderdale's location near the ocean affect my insurance rate?
Yes, significantly. Properties within 5 miles of the coast face higher windstorm risk, which is reflected in higher premiums. Properties in the High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) — which includes most of Broward County — are subject to stricter building code requirements and higher insurance rates. The closer your property is to the Intracoastal Waterway or the Atlantic Ocean, the higher your windstorm premium will typically be.
