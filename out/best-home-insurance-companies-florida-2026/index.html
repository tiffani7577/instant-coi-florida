Best Home Insurance Companies in Florida for 2026

The Florida insurance market looks nothing like it did five years ago. Here's an honest assessment of the carriers actually writing policies in 2026.

Get Free Quote

Recommending the "best" home insurance company in Florida in 2026 requires a different conversation than it did five years ago. Many of the national carriers that dominated the market — State Farm, Allstate, Farmers — have dramatically reduced or eliminated their Florida presence. The market is now dominated by Florida-specific carriers, Citizens Insurance, and a growing surplus lines market. Here's an honest assessment of your real options.

The State of Florida's Insurance Market in 2026

Florida's insurance market has been through a historic crisis. Between 2020 and 2024, six Florida insurance companies became insolvent, and dozens more stopped writing new policies or exited the state entirely. The causes are well-documented: hurricane losses, litigation abuse (now partially addressed by 2022-2023 legislative reforms), rising reinsurance costs, and the compounding effects of climate change on Florida's hurricane risk.

The 2022 and 2023 legislative reforms — which eliminated one-way attorney fees and assignment of benefits (AOB) in insurance claims — have begun to stabilize the market. Several new carriers have entered Florida, and existing carriers have modestly expanded their underwriting. But the market remains challenging, particularly for older homes, coastal properties, and homes with prior claims.

Citizens Insurance: The Insurer of Last Resort

Citizens Property Insurance Corporation is Florida's state-backed insurer of last resort. As of 2026, Citizens insures approximately 1.2 million Florida policies — down from a peak of 1.4 million as the private market has partially stabilized.

Who Citizens is right for: Homeowners who cannot find comparable private market coverage within 20% of Citizens' premium, owners of older homes that don't qualify for private market coverage, and homeowners with prior claims that make them unattractive to private carriers.

Citizens' strengths: State backing provides financial stability, coverage is available for properties that private carriers won't touch, and Citizens' rates are regulated and generally competitive for high-risk properties.

Citizens' limitations: Maximum coverage of $700,000 for single-family homes, policyholders are subject to assessments if Citizens becomes insolvent, and Citizens may be more expensive than private market alternatives for homes with favorable features.

Universal Property & Casualty

Universal P&C is one of Florida's largest private home insurers and has remained active in the state throughout the crisis. They write policies across most of Florida and have a range of products for different property types and risk profiles.

Best for: Homes with newer roofs and good wind mitigation features, where Universal's underwriting can be competitive with Citizens.

Watch for: Universal has raised rates significantly in recent years. Shop carefully and compare their quote against Citizens and other private carriers.

Heritage Insurance

Heritage Insurance Holdings is a Florida-based carrier that has maintained a significant presence in the state and has been actively writing new policies in Broward County and other South Florida markets.

Best for: Homeowners in Broward, Palm Beach, and Miami-Dade counties looking for private market alternatives to Citizens.

Slide Insurance

Slide Insurance is a newer Florida carrier that has grown rapidly since its founding in 2021. Slide has been actively taking on policies from Citizens through Florida's "depopulation" program and has expanded its underwriting to include new business.

Best for: Homeowners with newer homes and good wind mitigation features who want a private market alternative to Citizens.

Watch for: As a newer carrier, Slide has less track record through a major hurricane season than established carriers. Their financial strength rating should be monitored.

Kin Insurance

Kin is a technology-focused insurance startup that has been growing in Florida. They use data analytics to underwrite properties more precisely, which can result in competitive rates for homes with favorable risk profiles.

Best for: Tech-savvy homeowners with newer, well-maintained homes who want a streamlined digital experience.

Security First Financial

Security First is a Florida-based carrier that has maintained a consistent presence in the state. They have a range of products and are generally competitive for standard Florida home insurance.

How to Choose the Right Carrier for Your Home

The "best" carrier for your home depends on your specific property, location, and risk profile. Here's the process for finding the right fit:

  • Get a wind mitigation inspection first. Your wind mitigation report significantly affects which carriers will quote you and at what price. Have it done before you shop.
  • Work with an independent agent. An independent agent can access quotes from multiple carriers simultaneously and knows which carriers are currently competitive for your specific property.
  • Get at least 3 quotes. Premium differences between carriers for the same property can be $2,000-$4,000 per year.
  • Check financial strength ratings. Look for A.M. Best ratings of A- or better. A carrier that can't pay claims is worse than no carrier at all.
  • Read the coverage terms. The cheapest policy is not always the best value. Make sure you understand what's covered and what's excluded.
  • Shop every year. The Florida market changes constantly. The best carrier for your home today may not be the best carrier next year.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which home insurance companies are still writing policies in Florida in 2026?
Despite the market crisis, several carriers are actively writing new policies in Florida in 2026. These include Universal Property & Casualty, Citizens Insurance, Heritage Insurance, Slide Insurance, Security First Financial, Kin Insurance, and several surplus lines carriers. National carriers like State Farm and Allstate have significantly reduced their Florida presence. The market is dominated by Florida-specific carriers and Citizens Insurance.
Is Citizens Insurance a good option in Florida?
Citizens Insurance is a legitimate, financially stable option for many Florida homeowners. It is backed by the state of Florida and is required by law to pay valid claims. The main downsides are: Citizens policies may be more expensive than private market alternatives for homes with favorable features, Citizens has coverage limitations (maximum $700,000 for single-family homes), and Citizens policyholders are subject to assessments if Citizens becomes insolvent after a major hurricane. For many Florida homeowners, particularly those with older homes or prior claims, Citizens is the best available option.
Why did State Farm and Allstate stop writing new policies in Florida?
State Farm stopped writing new homeowners policies in Florida in 2023, citing the state's challenging insurance environment — specifically the high cost of reinsurance, litigation costs, and hurricane risk. Allstate has also significantly reduced its Florida presence. These decisions reflect the broader challenge of profitably insuring Florida homes at rates that consumers can afford. National carriers with shareholders to answer to have been less willing to absorb Florida's losses than state-backed Citizens or Florida-specific carriers.
What should I look for when choosing a Florida home insurance company?
Key factors to evaluate: (1) Financial strength rating — look for A.M. Best ratings of A- or better, (2) Florida-specific claims experience — how does the carrier handle Florida hurricane claims?, (3) Coverage terms — what's included and excluded?, (4) Premium stability — has the carrier been raising rates dramatically?, (5) Availability in your specific county and for your property type, (6) Customer service and claims handling reputation.
How often should I switch home insurance companies in Florida?
You should shop your coverage every year, 60-90 days before your renewal date. You should switch whenever you find meaningfully better coverage at a lower price from a financially stable carrier. In Florida's volatile market, the best carrier for your home can change year to year. Loyalty to a carrier rarely pays off in Florida — the market rewards active shoppers.
